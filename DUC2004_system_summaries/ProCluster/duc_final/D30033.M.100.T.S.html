The 11 countries adopting the euro as a common currency In a surprise move, the 11 European nations launching a common currency on Jan. 1 cut interest rates. The European Union and the United States will join forces to create the euro. The European Central Bank will set monetary policy for the euro area. The Bank of France pressed for rate cuts to help boost growth and combat unemployment. The European Central Bank suggested that governments should reform rigid labor markets to create jobs. The French and German stock exchanges have indicated they would be interested in joining the London and Frankfurt alliance
